Short Bio and Current Bearings

Greetings!


Well, this is me: A GIS Analyst, ex-archaeologist, and IT developer.
From two years on, I devoted myself to web development projects, getting acquainted with front-end (HTML, CSS, JS, Bootstrap, Handlebars) and some back-end (Maven, Node.js, PHP, SQL) technologies, which provided me with a general view of the field and a "junior" working capacity. However, no proper job position achieved so far. Nevertheless, this last year I have expanded my IT skills to the GeoWeb ecosystem by enroling on a MSc in GIS Technologies. This provided me with an excellent update on my traditional GIS skills, and, more importantly, on a new array of tech and skills directly connected to web development and data analysis. Thus, I am competent in using open data (geospatial data, actually), APIs (Google Maps, MapboxGL), and web mapping libraries (leaflet.js) to provide geospatial capabilities and data to web and mobile apps.


Before that I have been on the Archaeologist track for about twenty years. My interest on IT tech was already there since Archaeological research moves right behind cutting-edge scientific research tech and methods. Then, I learned about something called GIS and spatial analysis applied to Archaeology, which entailed amazing research possibilities. From there I got involved in data management, databases, SQL sentences, and thematic cartography. In short: a mixed profile composed by humanistic perspective and technological skills focused in spatial-related solutions for research questions. Thanks to that I have worked in a number of spatial-related projects, both in the academic world, and in engineering and building companies. In sum, I can proudly say that I had a rich and productive career, crowned by a PhD in Archaeology.


But times change, my path was stalling, and I decided to go with the times and swapped careers. I brainstormed for a littel while and realize that tech had been a constant since the beginng. Moreover, and as happens today, tech was already everywhere. The choice seemed rather obvious for me, and now, here I am!
Well, this is my story. But, in order to make your own impression of me, please, just go and check my profile and stuff located through the links below. I am sure you can figure out what sort of professional I am. And, in the lucky case that anything catches your eye, please, send me your feedback. Again, thanks for coming by and best wishes!
